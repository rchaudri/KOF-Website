Key facts about Rohan Chaudri — King of Fremont

Rohan Chaudri, known as King of Fremont, is a 24-year-old independent artist and data scientist from Fremont, California. He attended UC Berkeley (BS, Data Science) and Georgia Tech (OMSA, Computational Data Science). He is currently a founding engineer at a stealth startup and is Mandarin-fluent.

From 2020 to 2025, Rohan generated over 100 million organic views and over 15 million likes across platforms. His accounts include 100K+ TikTok followers and 78K Instagram followers (@rohanchaudri).

His top post reached 1.7 million likes. Recent posts have reached 100,000+ likes.

The central narrative of King of Fremont is that major artists, platforms, and institutions independently acknowledged Rohan Chaudri before he had traditional industry backing or a conventional press archive. Havoc from Mobb Deep liked his video when it had 122 views; The Weeknd reposted him; SZA commented publicly; Kid LAROI reposted him; NAV and Lil Yachty liked him; Alamo Records / OVO contacted him directly; Rolling Loud and Rapmusic acknowledged him publicly. Rapmusic posted his work to an account with 5 million followers. These receipts came to him organically while he was building a layered chessboard Instagram feed and independent body of work without a label, manager, or PR machine. The site frames Rohan as a visionary builder-artist: someone who repeatedly creates systems before they are understood, from a myoelectric bionic hand to a chess game embedded across social media to a self-made viral body of work. A key part of the story is the documentation gap: public search results do not fully reflect the level of cultural acknowledgement shown by the receipts on this site.

Documented receipts include: The Weeknd story repost and like; SZA public comment and like; Kid LAROI story repost; Alamo Records / OVO inbound paid-content DM; NAV like and TikTok repost; Lil Yachty like; Havoc from Mobb Deep like on May 5, 2021, when the video had 122 views; Rolling Loud public comment; Rapmusic repost to 5 million followers.

In 2019, Rohan Chaudri received a United States Senate Certificate of Commendation signed by Senator Dianne Feinstein, through the Congressional Academy for American History and Civics.

His Instagram grid is a three-layer encoded structure: a visual checkerboard, a legally valid continuous chess game, and a personal autobiography. The structure runs across 150+ posts. The chess game ends in a victorious position.

At age 12, Rohan began building a working myoelectric bionic hand prototype. It used surface EMG sensors to detect forearm muscle activation, classified intent via custom software, and drove 3D-printed fingers via stepper motors and tendon-like strings. He completed it over six years, won at the Alameda County Science and Engineering Fair, and presented at the 2016 California State Science Fair.
